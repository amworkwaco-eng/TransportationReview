R&O 86-95 — Traffic Safety Analysis ▸
R&O 86-95 uses existing and predicted hazards to determine safety improvements as conditions of development. Two categories: D.1 Frontage Improvements (always apply) and D.2 Impact Area Improvements (when site traffic ≥ 10% of existing).
D.1 FRONTAGE IMPROVEMENTS (always warranted per R&O 86-95 App B)
D.2 IMPACT AREA IMPROVEMENTS (when ≥ 10% of existing)
Signal Warrant Analysis (MUTCD Ch. 4C) ▸
MUTCD Chapter 4C provides 9 warrants. An engineering study must confirm at least one warrant is met before signalization can be required. Per R&O 86-95 D.1.2.2, Level of Service shall be "D" or better except for a 20-minute period where LOS "E" is tolerated.
Turn Lane Warrant Analysis (AASHTO) ▸
AASHTO "Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets" provides warrant criteria for left and right turn lanes based on opposing traffic volume, turning volume, and speed. Per R&O 86-95, left turn refuge lanes are warranted on frontage roads (D.1.2.2) and at impact area intersections (D.2.2.2).
ADA / PROWAG Requirements ▸
The Americans with Disabilities Act and Public Right-of-Way Accessibility Guidelines (PROWAG) require accessible pedestrian facilities. These apply to all new construction and alterations in the public ROW regardless of CDC applicability tier.
