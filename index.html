DOCUMENT HIERARCHY
1. COMPREHENSIVE FRAMEWORK PLAN Parent Document
Role: Supreme local policy. All other documents must be consistent. Adopted by Board of Commissioners. Acknowledged by DLCD per ORS 197.251.
Contains: Community Plans, Area of Special Concern policies, urban/rural boundaries.
Adopts → TSP (as element) · Implemented by → CDC · Supported by → Community Plans
▼
2. TRANSPORTATION SYSTEM PLAN Child of Comp Plan — Policy
Role: 20-year transportation policy element. Establishes functional classifications, Table 3 design parameters, modal elements, goals/objectives/strategies.
Legal basis: Goal 12, TPR (OAR 660-012), Metro RTFP.
Key link: CDC §501-8.4 requires ROW "pursuant to TSP classification and Road Standards." TSP classification = bridge between policy and technical design.
Parent → Comp Plan · Implemented by → CDC §501-8 · Technical standards → Road Design Stds
▼
3. COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT CODE Implements Comp Plan & TSP — Regulatory
Role: Regulatory code implementing the Comp Plan. Article V = transportation chapter. Determines what can be conditioned — the legal authority for exactions.
Critical: Without a CDC section authorizing a requirement, it cannot be imposed regardless of Road Standards. This is where Nollan/Dolan/Sheetz applies.
Key: §501-8.8 A lists 12 half-street elements. §130.030 lists 11 (CDC adds ped/bike crossings). CDC controls per Road Std §120.010.
Parent → Comp Plan · References → TSP classifications · Authorizes → Road Stds · References → R&O 86-95
▼
4. ROAD DESIGN & CONSTRUCTION STANDARDS Technical — Younger Sibling of CDC
Role: Technical engineering manual. Specifies cross-sections (Exhibits 1-6), structural design, drainage, construction methods. WCCO Title 15 Ch. 15.08.
Limitation: Cannot expand what CDC authorizes. If CDC doesn't authorize half-street for a use type, the Exhibit cross-section is irrelevant to conditioning.
Relationship: "Younger sibling" — adopted separately, implements same parent policies. CDC controls what; Road Stds control how. When they conflict, CDC controls (§120.010).
Parent policy → Comp Plan + TSP · Authority → CDC Article V · Implements → TSP Table 3 (Exhibits 1-6)
The gap that creates legal risk: an engineering requirement from Road Standards that has no CDC section authorizing it for the specific application type. The Authority Map tab flags these gaps.
